## Brand Position

Bengula Inc is a decision-intelligence and advisory platform focused on
finance, technology, business growth, trade, and economic systems in East
Africa. It helps businesses, investors, and professionals make better
decisions by combining finance, technology, and strategic analysis into
practical guidance. The brand voice should make complex money, trade, and
growth decisions feel understandable without making them feel simplistic.

The Bengula Research Desk explains the systems, incentives, and risks
behind business, finance, technology, and economic change so readers can
act with greater confidence.

[table widths=42,128]
Element | Guidance
Core promise | Practical clarity for business growth, capital access, and investment decisions.
Tone | Clear, composed, precise, optimistic, and evidence-led. A trusted analyst, not a salesperson.
Avoid | Guaranteed-return language, hype, secrets, sensationalism, excessive luxury cues, jargon without explanation, and overly casual finance claims.
Signature line | Adding meaning to life.
Audience | Kenyan and East African SMEs, founders, professionals, investors, cooperatives, diaspora clients, and decision makers seeking evidence-led guidance.
[/table]

## Editorial North Star

Every piece of content should reduce uncertainty for the reader. Readers
should leave knowing what matters, what risks exist, and what actions are
available. Content that informs without helping the reader decide is not
finished.

> positive: The test of any Bengula asset is simple: did it move the
> reader from information to a clearer decision?

## Voice & Tone Mix

Bengula writing blends five registers in deliberate proportion. The mix
keeps analysis at the centre and promotion at the margin.

[table widths=36,24,110]
Register | Weight | What it sounds like
Analytical | 35% | Explains causes, mechanisms, incentives, and trade-offs with evidence.
Practical | 30% | Converts analysis into steps, checklists, thresholds, and next actions.
Educational | 20% | Defines terms plainly and builds the reader's mental model.
Strategic | 10% | Connects today's facts to long-term positioning and second-order effects.
Promotional | 5% | Points to Bengula services only where they genuinely fit the reader's need.
[/table]

## Editorial Principles

- Clarity over complexity: simplify the language, never the thinking.
- Evidence over opinion: claims carry data, sources, or worked numbers.
- Insight over information: reveal something non-obvious, not a news recap.
- Long-term thinking over short-term reactions.
- Explain systems, not just events: show why things happen, not only that they happened.

## Article Structure

Long-form articles and research PDFs follow a six-part arc:

1. Hook: a sharp opening fact, tension, or question that earns attention.
2. Context: what the reader needs to know to follow the analysis.
3. Analysis: the mechanism — causes, incentives, and evidence.
4. Implications: what this means for the reader's money, business, or strategy.
5. Recommendations: concrete, prioritised actions with their trade-offs.
6. Conclusion: tie back to the reader's goals; one memorable takeaway.

## The Bengula Lens

Every article examines its subject through the same analytical lens.
Before publishing, check that the piece has addressed each angle.

[cards]
Incentives :: Who gains, who pays, and what behaviour does the structure reward?
Constraints :: What limits the actors involved — capital, regulation, liquidity, infrastructure, execution capacity?
Risks :: What can go wrong, how likely is it, and who carries the loss when it does?
Opportunities :: Where does the change create openings for readers positioned to act?
Trade-offs :: What does each course of action cost, and what is given up by choosing it?
[/cards]

## Signature Sections

Recurring, named sections make Bengula content recognisable and easy to
scan. Use them consistently across articles, PDFs, and decks.

[cards]
Key Insight :: The single non-obvious conclusion of the piece, stated early and plainly.
Risk Factors :: An honest list of what can go wrong, with severity and likelihood where possible.
Decision Framework :: A short checklist or table the reader can apply to their own situation.
Bengula View :: The desk's position — what we would weigh most heavily, and why.
[/cards]

## The Publication Test

Before any asset ships, it must pass four questions. If the answer to any
is no, the piece goes back for another pass.

1. Does it reveal something non-obvious?
2. Does it explain causes rather than symptoms?
3. Does it help readers make better decisions?
4. Does it connect information to action?

> risk: Content that fails the test but ships anyway erodes the advisory
> positioning the brand depends on. When in doubt, cut the piece, not the
> standard.

carries authority and brand recognition, slate carries text and structure,

## Signature Effects

The website pairs the palette with a liquid-glass aesthetic: frosted white
cards over a softly tinted canvas, an aurora backdrop of blurred violet,
orchid, and sky gradients, and a panning violet-to-indigo gradient on
headline text. In static formats, echo this with light backgrounds, soft
violet shadows, rounded corners, and a single dark-violet cover moment.

- Glass cards: white at roughly 62 percent opacity with a hairline border and a soft violet shadow.
- Gradient text: Electric Violet to Orchid to Indigo Pulse, reserved for one headline per asset.
- Dark panels: Deep Violet with light violet text, used sparingly for contrast moments.

## Typography

[table widths=36,50,84]
Role | Recommended font | Usage
Headlines | Georgia or Charter | Editorial, credible, advisory. Use for PDF covers, article titles, and major section headings.
Body/UI | Helvetica Neue, Inter, or Segoe UI | Clean, highly readable, modern. Use for web, decks, tables, captions, and body copy.
Numbers/data | JetBrains Mono or Aptos Mono | Use sparingly for rates, tickers, tags, IDs, and compact dashboards.
Fallback | Helvetica | Use where platform compatibility matters more than character.
[/table]

- Do not use more than two font families in one asset unless data tables need a mono style.
- Headlines should be confident and plain, not slogan-heavy.
- Tables should use smaller type only when spacing remains comfortable.

## Layout Rules

- Use generous white space and a strong typographic hierarchy before adding decoration.
- Use tables only for true comparisons, checklists, or data. Use prose for explanation.
- Advisory-grade presentation: every marketing PDF includes a cover, executive summary, practical framework, source list, and compliance note.
