Marketing requires one to have a good knowledge of the customer and the market. A good customer avatar gives you a superhuman ability to communicate powerfully to prospective customers. It can help your business sell more products, reach a bigger audience and grow your list of customers.
The main aim of the avatar is to figure out your ideal customer. In a general sense, avatars are often fictional figures or graphical illustrations that represent a specific individual. Customer avatars, also called a buyer persona or marketing persona, help to narrow down particular characteristics of the people buying your products.
The avatar helps understand the needs of your ideal customers and clientele. The buyer persona has all the characteristics, such as the pains and wants of your customer. The character helps you reach the correct market as you no longer have to waste resources marketing to a clique that won’t convert to customers. Businesses can have more than one avatar to meet more than one market. Many businesses do not have avatars despite running for long.
### How to Create a Business Avatar
Creating an avatar, in essence, is not coming up with a new thing but discovering your ideal client and finding a way of targeting and solving the customers’ problems. The process is much simpler for more established businesses as they can leverage the data they have but more critical for new businesses which need to boost business and meet the right market. The process is data intensive and may need you to collect data to help you identify your ideal client. Available data from marketing campaign results, interviews, and surveys can make it much easier to make a buyer persona for your business. You can use customer avatar templates to help you get started.
- Identify your Customer’s Dreams and Goals
A name may seem very inconsequential but carries a lot of weight. It makes it easier to reference your avatar and cross-team consistency. Say you sell engineering components to your clients, or you are a business coach that offers courses to business leaders.
In the first instance, it is better to name your avatar engineer John while in the second instance, you could call the avatar CEO John. You can lump closely related fields and customers into one, such as business managers and entrepreneurs, into the CEO category.
The goals in this section are not your business goals, and you should not aim to project your goals on the avatar. Take your time to learn your customer’s aspirations and what they want to achieve in the long term and short term. Set out short-term and long-term questions to help you accurately determine your clients’ goals. List down goals and values relevant to your business proposition. Use the goal to determine how you fit into their lives and how you can help solve the problems. You are free to use social media to reach out to your customers in this step too and pay attention to the customer your customer is asking and the things they pay attention to.
- Find Customer Avatar's Sources of Information
Narrow to the Source of Information of your customer avatar so you can really target your marketing campaign. You have to be able to figure out the kinds of books or magazines your customers are reading. If they love blogs, what kind of blogs do they read. The aim of narrowing down their source of information is so you can meet and get the kind of message that resonates with them. It will also be effective in making content strategy and deciding whether to use Email Marketing, blogs, or other means to reach them.
- Segment the Demographic of the Customer Avatar
The key to coming up with a spot-on customer avatar is to have an entire list of characteristics and traits of your customer since generalizations do not really work. Use demographics to identify the type of customer that buys your product and psychographics to gauge the reasons the customers buy your product.
Demographics usually classify people based on their age, income, sex, habits, gender, income, location, and job status, among other characteristics that may be relevant to the business. Psychographics help you get into the shoes of the customer to understand their values, beliefs, and what they enjoy. You do want to offer goods and services that offer a unique value proposition to the customer and do not go against their values. The more you can understand the customer, the better you can develop the marketing persona.
You can use statistical data and marketing tools to analyze the data to further refine your knowledge of the customer. If it is possible to run your data through an AI or let professionals help you in conducting a survey, the better. Any information can lead you on and make your marketing campaign successful. The more data points you have on the customer, the more opportunities you can interact with them.

While more information is generally better, not all information may be relevant for your business. You have to be particularly careful when using customer avatar templates to generate a buyer persona. Decide on the demographics and psychographics that seem relevant to your business.
Reach out to your clients and talk to them to have a more accurate picture of who they are and how they feel. Knowing the notions of your client helps you reach their level and create marketing content that guides them to make contact with your business. Feel free to use polls on social media to gather information on your client.
- Identify Your Customer's Problems and pain points
Having identified their goals, it is good to also find out the challenges they face. Set out to find out all the pain points, you should be aware that most of them may not leave their feedback online, and rigorous data collection and analysis can take you closer to this goal. The challenges you are after are the ones you can provide a solution for and your business idea.
Successfully identifying your customer’s challenges will help you generate a great product that solves their needs. Dive deeper by asking clients the why questions. Find out why your customer avatar may or may not buy products from you.
#### 4. Find out Customer Role in The Sale process and their Objections
All products have strong points and weak points. It is in your best interest to find the product’s weak points and why the client avatar may have objections to buying your product. Find ways of countering their argument.
When you have the avatar ready, it is time to find out how you can solve the avatar’s problems. You should aim to have the right offer messaging and place to increase conversions in your marketing campaigns.
